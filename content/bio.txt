Hello! By some strange twist of fate, you've arrived at my personal website. 
I'm Eli. I build software for researchers at the [University of Michigan](https://umich.edu) to
help them focus on their domain expertise instead of technical details. I'm 
lucky to work on projects that range from tracking near-term risk factors of 
suicide to analyzing whole slide images of cancer tissue through machine learning
data pipelines. 

My background is in psychology and I spent 14 years as a master's-level psychologist
while writing code and building software as a hobby (like [this website](https://github.com/elitwilson/elitwilson.github.io)
built in Rust and Ratatui). Now I'm fortunate enough to blend
my two passions into a career, building things that hopefully leave the world a better 
place than I found it. 

I enjoy the rapid pace of technological advancement, and try to be as close as I can
to the bleeding edge of the latest tech and newest trends. I've spent a lot of time
thinking about and developing my own agentic workflows, tools, and skills to help me
leverage AI and modern tooling to be more productive while maintaining and respecting
the irreplaceable value of human ownership and creativity.
